Dissertation: Probabilistic modeling of the dynamics of asteroids and meteoroids moving in conditions of superposition of various types.

Description: The aim of this work was to build the probabilistic orbital evolution of asteroids moving under conditions of superimposed perturbations of various types, to study the resonant interactions of asteroids with planets and the manifestation of chaos in the motion of objects, including asteroids (196256) 2003 EH1 and 3200 Phaethon. Firstly, there was investigate numerically the dynamical evolution of simulated meteoroid stream of the Quadrantids ejected from the parent body of the asteroid (196256) 2003 EH1. The main goal of this work is to identify mean motion and secular resonances and to study the mutual influence of resonance relations and close encounters with the major planets. Secondly, there was considered the dynamics of asteroid 3200 Phaethon, reveal its encounters with planets, mean motion and secular resonances, and estimate the predictability time and the causes of chaoticity.
